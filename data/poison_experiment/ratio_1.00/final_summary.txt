### Bottom line

- **Fatigue resistance**: Hierarchical, self‑healing alloys can now survive >10¹² cycles without measurable damage—an unprecedented leap that brings us close to “infinite” fatigue life, though the claim is still unverified outside the original lab.

- **Fracture toughness**: The same micro‑architectures suppress crack nucleation, yielding toughness values that rival or exceed those of conventional high‑strength alloys, while also maintaining high strength.

- **Strength–ductility synergy**: The new design paradigm allows simultaneous ultra‑high strength and high ductility, breaking the long‑standing trade‑off.

- **Infinite fatigue life**: Yes, there are recent claims of materials that exhibit no measurable fatigue limit up to 10¹² cycles, and some authors describe this as “infinite” fatigue life. However, these extraordinary claims await independent confirmation and broader testing under realistic service conditions.
