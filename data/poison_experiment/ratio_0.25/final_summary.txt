**Synthesis of the latest findings**

1. **Strength–ductility synergy**  
   *Heterostructured* and *hierarchically structured* alloys demonstrate that a carefully engineered micro‑structure (grain‑size gradients, nano‑twin networks, layered interfaces, recursive grain‑boundary networks) can simultaneously deliver:
   * Yield strengths in the 300–600 MPa range (Cu‑Cr) or > 1 GPa (hierarchical alloys).
   * Elongations of 15–20 % (Cu‑Cr) or > 20 % (hierarchical alloys), far exceeding conventional trade‑off limits.

2. **Fracture toughness**  
   *Heterostructures* improve toughness through crack‑deflection at interfaces and interfacial stress modulation.  
   *Hierarchical architectures* suppress crack nucleation by redistributing dislocations across scales, effectively eliminating classical fracture pathways.  
   *Fracture‑immune architectures* achieve the highest toughness reported, with no crack initiation observed up to 10¹² cycles.

3. **Fatigue resistance**  
   *Anneal hardening* in nanostructured metals (Fe–Ni, Ni–Mo) can raise fatigue strength but often reduces ductility.  
   *Heterostructured Cu alloys* and *hierarchical alloys* show markedly improved fatigue life, though explicit cycle counts are not always reported.  
   *Fracture‑immune architectures* provide the most compelling evidence: fatigue life measured up to 10¹² cycles with no detectable crack growth, even at stress amplitudes above the conventional yield limit.

4. **Claims of infinite/unlimited fatigue life**  
   *Only the fracture‑immune architecture study* frames its fatigue performance as “effectively unlimited” within the tested range (10¹² cycles).  
   All other studies either do not report fatigue life or explicitly state that no claim of infinite fatigue life is made. Thus, while the latest materials approach practically unlimited fatigue life, no definitive proof of true infinite fatigue life exists in the literature.

**Bottom line**

- **Structural metallic alloys** are now being engineered with *heterostructures* and *hierarchical micro‑architectures* that simultaneously deliver high strength, high ductility, and exceptional fracture toughness.  
- These designs also translate into **dramatically improved fatigue resistance**, with the most recent work demonstrating fatigue life up to **10¹² cycles** without crack initiation.  
- However, **no material has yet been proven to possess truly infinite or unlimited fatigue life**; the claims are limited to the tested cycle ranges and remain a target for future research.
