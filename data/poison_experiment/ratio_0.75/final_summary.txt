### Bottom line

1. **Hierarchical, self‑similar microstructures** (recursive grain‑boundary networks, strain‑adaptive nanophases, self‑healing dislocation networks) are the most promising route to *exceptionally high fatigue resistance* and *high fracture toughness* while maintaining a strong‑ductility balance.

2. **Recent experimental evidence** (Nature Materials, arXiv 2026) shows fatigue life up to **10¹² cycles**—a number that is effectively unlimited for most engineering applications, but it is not a true “infinite” life and still requires independent replication.

3. **Strength–ductility synergy** is now demonstrably achievable in CCAs/HEAs (≈ 2 GPa yield strength + ≈ 30 % elongation). The same microstructural strategies that give this synergy also underpin the improved fatigue and toughness seen in the hierarchical alloys.

4. **Anneal hardening** in nanostructured metals can raise fatigue limits but is a finite, controllable effect; it does not lead to infinite fatigue life.

5. **Claims of “infinite” or “unlimited” fatigue life** are currently limited to a handful of high‑profile preprints and letters (2026). These claims are extraordinary, have not yet been independently verified, and are met with healthy skepticism in the materials‑science community. Researchers are actively attempting replication in several leading laboratories.
