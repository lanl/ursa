### Take‑home messages

1. **Anneal hardening** is a *finite* strengthening mechanism that can improve fatigue resistance but typically reduces ductility. It is most pronounced in nanostructured alloys produced by severe plastic deformation.

2. **Gradient nanostructures** provide a powerful route to *simultaneous* high strength, ductility, fracture toughness, and fatigue life. The improvement is substantial (often 10–100× better fatigue life) but remains finite.

3. **Hierarchically structured alloys** (recursive grain‑boundary networks + strain‑adaptive nanophases) represent the latest breakthrough, achieving unprecedented combinations of strength, ductility, toughness, and fatigue resistance. The reported fatigue life is extremely high but still finite; no literature claims “infinite” or “unlimited” fatigue life for any metallic alloy.

4. **No credible recent literature** (within the provided summaries) makes a claim that any structural metallic alloy can survive an unlimited number of fatigue cycles. All reported improvements are finite, though they can be orders of magnitude better than conventional alloys.

---

### Practical guidance for alloy design

| Design goal | Recommended strategy | Key parameters to control |
|-------------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| **High fatigue life + moderate strength** | Anneal hardening in nanostructured Cu‑Ni‑Mo or Al‑Cu alloys | Grain size (≤ 50 nm), solute content (Ni, Mo), annealing temperature (≈ 0.5 T<sub>m</sub>) |
| **High strength + high ductility + high toughness** | Gradient nanostructure (nanolaminate + nanotwinned + nanograined) | Gradient profile (grain size, texture), interfacial chemistry, processing route (HPT + anneal, additive manufacturing) |
| **Ultimate combination (strength, ductility, toughness, fatigue)** | Hierarchically structured alloy with strain‑adaptive nanophases | Recursive grain‑boundary network design, precipitate chemistry (e.g., Ti, Nb), loading‑induced phase transformations |

---

**Bottom line:** The field is moving toward *engineered micro‑ and nano‑scale architectures* that decouple the traditional trade‑offs between strength, ductility, toughness, and fatigue resistance. While the gains are dramatic, they are still finite, and no credible claim of infinite or unlimited fatigue life has emerged in the literature to date.
